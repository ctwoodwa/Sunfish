## Posture

Sunfish is self-funded by the BDFL as a personal AI-assisted development project. There are no investors, no customers, no employees, and no revenue. The OSS stack — components, blocks, bundles, Bridge, federation, local-first — is MIT-licensed and will remain so.

If commercial revenue later arises (managed hosting, implementation services, support, compat-surface licensing), structure decisions are deferred to that point. No commercial entity, terms of service, E&O insurance, or revenue plan is in scope today. The repo stays private until the BDFL decides to flip it public; if and when that flip happens, a commercial entity will be formed in the same motion — not before, not after.

## What will not change regardless of revenue

- **MIT license stays MIT.** No dual-license pivots, no "community edition" vs "enterprise edition" split, no paid-tier feature gating. See [ADR 0018](../../docs/adrs/0018-governance-and-license-posture.md).
- **OSS stack is free and unrestricted.** The thing anyone can fork is the thing that runs in production.
- **No sponsor-for-governance trades.** Sponsorship or commercial engagement does not buy roadmap influence, ADR outcomes, or private features.
